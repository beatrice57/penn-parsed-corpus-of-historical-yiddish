Annotation guidelines for the Penn Parsed Corpus of Historical Yiddish

Unless otherwise specified below, the Penn Parsed Corpus of Historical Yiddish is annotated according to the guidelines for the Penn Parsed Historical Corpora of English (PPCHE).

Splitting and joining

In orthographic words that need to be split to conform to the parsing guidelines (notably particle-verb combinations), the site of the split is indicated by a trailing "@" on the first item and a leading "@" on the second one. 
( (PP (P mit@)
      (NP (D @n) (N andern))))

( (RP oyf@) (TO @tsu@) (VB @shteyn))

Conversely, sequences of words that are ordinarily spelled as a single orthographic word are joined with an underscore. 

( (ADVP (ADV eyn_zu)))

( (VBN fr_zamlt))

Metalinguistic material

All punctuation is uniformly tagged PUNC. In order to facilitate interaction with Unix, exclamation points are rendeded as "%EXCL%". Otherwise, the relevant PPCHE guidelines apply to the following tags.

CODE, ID, LS, META, REF

FOREIGN domimates sequences of two or more FW tags.

Linguistic examples in discussions of Yiddish grammar (1927e-zaretski-sholem, 1928e-zaretski-mendele, 1932e-shtif, 1946e-mark) are marked as such by the suffix -EXAMPLE on the appropriate label (IP-MAT-EXAMPLE, and so on).

Free translations from Hebrew or other languages are marked as such with the suffix -TRANSL on the appropriate label (IP-MAT-TRANSL, NP-TRANSL, and so on). See below for word-for-word glosses and translations.

Finally, material of the following types is marked with an IGNORE- prefix:

  • IGNORE-ANACHRONISM - quotations in Modern Yiddish sources of Early Yiddish material
  • IGNORE-TRANSL - word-for-word glosses or translations of Hebrew material (kheyder-taytsh)
  • IGNORE-UNGRAMM - ungrammatical examples in discussions of Yiddish grammar

Disfluencies and parentheticals

Elaborations are sometimes annotated in this corpus with the extended tag -PRN rather than with ELAB. Other disfluences are very rare and annotated as FS and REP as required.

Specific topics

CP-QUE-MAT-THT (indirect question with inversion)

Indirect questions are generally annotated as CP-QUE-SUB. But in cases where they exhibit the word order of direct questions (with subject-verb inversion), they are annotated as CP-QUE-MAT on a par with direct questions, but with the added dash tag -THT to indicate their complement status (analogously to the case of IP-MAT-THT). 
( (IP-MAT (NP-SBJ (PRO me))
          (HVP hot)
          (VBN gemakht)
          (NP-ACC (D an) (N asife))
          (PP (P in)
              (NP (N shul)))
          (PUNC ,)
          (CP-QUE-MAT-THT (WNP-1 (WPRO vemen))
                          (IP-SUB (NP-ACC *T*-1)
                                  (MDP zol)
                                  (NP-SBJ (PRO me))
                                  (ADVP (ADV do))
                                  (VB shikn)))
          (PUNC .))
  (ID 1947E-ROYTE-POMERANTSEN,220.5580))

( (IP-MAT (VBP freg)
          (NP-SBJ (PRO ikh))
	  (NP-DTV (D dem) (N fish))
          (PUNC ,)
          (CP-QUE-MAT-THT (C tsi)
                          (IP-SUB (HVP hot)
                                  (NP-SBJ (PRO er))
                                  (NEG nit)
                                  (VBN gezen)
                                  (PP (P in)
                                      (NP (N vaser)))
                                  (NP-ACC (D dem) (N yidn)))
                          (PUNC ?))
          (PUNC "))
  (ID 1947E-ROYTE-POMERANTSEN,47.1240))

Doubled constituent

In noun phrases conforming to the template "Det N Det N", the second "Det N" sequence is annotated as NP-DBL. The nouns in questions are sometimes tagged as adjectives, or are modified by adjectives. It is not always clear whether a noun phrase is doubled (-DBL) or an ordinary parenthetical (-PRN; the default (for ease of recovery) is -DBL. 
( (IP-MAT (CONJ ober)
          (NP-SBJ (NPR moyshe-rabeynu))
          (BEP iz)
          (ADVP-DIAGN (ADV dokh))
          (VBN geven)
          (NP-PRD (D a)
                  (N yid)
                  (NP-DBL (D a) (N khokhem)))
          (PUNC ,))
  (ID 1947E-ROYTE-POMERANTSEN,248.6440))
Note the contrast with instances containing only a single noun. 
( (NP-SBJ (D ayner) (D a) (N yid)))
Verbs can also be doubled. The verb in the noncanonical (generally, clause-initial) position is the one that counts as doubled. 
( (IP-MAT (VB-DBL hern)
          (HVP hob)
          (NP-SBJ (PRO ikh))
          (NP-ACC (PRO es))
          (NEG nit)
          (VBN gehert)
          (PUNC .))
  (ID 1946E-MARK,8.233))

( (CP-THT (C az)
          (IP-SUB (VP (VB-DBL trinken)
                      (NP-ACC (D di) (N tey)))
                  (HVP hot)
                  (NP-SBJ (PRO ir))
                  (VBN getrunken)
                  (PP (P mit)
                      (NP (PRO$ ayer) (ADJ eygene) (N tsuker))))))
  (ID 1947E-ROYTE-POMERANTSEN,186.4696))

-DIAGN

The tag -DIAGN distinguishes the sentential adverbs DEN, DOKH, EFSHER, MISTOME, NOCH, SHOYN, YO, which play the same role as sentence-level NEG in diagnosing whether the finite verb has moved to sentence-medial INFL (or possibly higher).

DR+P

In general, the corpus contains no compound tags ("plus" tags) to indicate word-internal structure. The sole exception concerns words consisting of a demonstrative or interrogative adverb (corresponding to German DA/WO or English THERE/WHERE) combined with a preposition or particle. The intent is to facilitate the analysis of selectional restrictions imposed by a governing verb. 
(PP (ADV+P dernokh))
(PP (ADV+P dr_mit))

(WPP (WADV+P warum))

Embedded V2

See the particular cases below:
  • CP-QUE-MAT-THT (indirect question with direct question word order)
  • IP-MAT-DEG (asyndetic degree clause)
  • IP-MAT-REL (integrated adnominal clause)
  • IP-MAT-THT (asyndetic complement clause)

ES

ES marks the overt counterpart (ES, S) of the silent expletive pronoun *exp*. Following IcePaHC, ES is used in the following constructions:

  • impersonal passive
  • existential construction, including transitive expletive construction
  • clausal extraposition - The expletive NP (generally a subject, but possibly an object) is coindexed with the extraposed clause. Extraposition is also possible in connection with demonstrative pronouns. Such pronouns are also coindexed with their clausal associates, but tagged as D rather than ES.

Hebrew material

Hebrew words are tagged as H, and a gloss is appended with a linking caret. Unknown glosses are indicated by UNKNOWN. 
(NP (H hbtsulh^the_young_woman))
(NP-ACC (D das) (H tbet^ring) (H zhb^UNKNOWN))

Hebrew bound morphemes are split only if attached to Germanic words, as sometimes occurs. 

(NP (H h@^the) (N @veg))

In general, Hebrew material does not have Yiddish sister constituents. In the few exceptions, the Hebrew material is not given a full parse, but the Yiddish material is annotated as if it had been. 

( (PP (H ltubh^to_good)
      (NP-COM (D di) (N yidn))))              ← actual annotation

( (PP (P l@)
      (NP (N @tubh)
	  (NP-COM (D di) (N yidn)))))         ← assumed full structure

IP-MAT-DEG

IP-MAT-DEG indicates a degree complement clause corresponding to CP-DEG, but with main clause word order. 
( (IP-MAT ...
          (NP-LGS (ADV azoy)
                  (D eyn)
                  (ADJ groys)
                  (N gishrey)
                  (CONJP (CONJ un)
                         (NX (N giveyn)))
                  (IP-MAT-DEG (CP-ADV (C zint)
                                      (IP-SUB (NP-SBJ (D di) (N velt))
                                              (VBP steyt)))
                              (BEP iz)
                              (ADVP (ADV nakh))
                              (NP-SBJ (ADV azoy) (D eyn) (N tsrh))
                              (NEG nit)
                              (VBN givezin)
			      (PP (P in)
                                  (NP (NPR pulin))))))
  (ID 1834E-UKRAINE-2,39.98))

IP-MAT-REL (integrated adnominal IP-MAT)

IP-MATs functioning as relative clauses are explicitly given a -REL dash tag to facilitate their retrieval. 
( (IP-MAT (NP-SBJ (PRO ikh))
          (VBP hab)
          (NP-ACC (D eyn)
                  (N kind)
                  (CODE {TEXT:kund})
                  (IP-MAT-REL (NP-SBJ *pro*)
                              (VBP heyst)
                              (NP-SPR (NPR itskhk))))
          (PUNC ...))
  (ID 1XXXX-COURT-TESTIMONY,198_c1648_e?.1297))

IP-MAT-THT

Clausal complements not introduced by an overt complementizer (asyndetic complement clauses) are annotated as IP-MAT-THT. 
( (IP-MAT (NP-SBJ (PRO ikh))
          (VBP hof)
          (IP-MAT-THT (NP-SBJ (PRO er))
                      (MDP zul)
                      (NP-DTV (PRO mir))
                      (VB gebn)))
  (ID 1XXXX-COURT-TESTIMONY,33_c1460_w?.127))

Multiple wh-

In the multiple wh- constructions in the corpus, the wh-phrases are all fronted, and the IP dominates multiple wh- traces. 
( (CP-QUE-SUB (WADVP-1 (WADV vi))
              (WNP-2 (WPRO ver))
              (IP-SUB (NP-SBJ *T*-2)
                      (ADVP *T*-1)
                      (VBP lakht)))
  (ID 1947E-ROYTE-POMERANTSEN,1.3))

NP-COM

NP-COM marks complements of adjectives, nouns, numerals, and quantifiers. These tend to follow their head, but may precede, especially in Early Yiddish. Possessives are marked separately (NP-POS). 
( (NP (D a)
      (N shtik)
      (NP-COM (N broyt))))

( (NP (NP-COM (PRO$ irr))
      (NUM tsvey)))

In noun phrases expressing a dimension and a measure unit, the measure unit is treated as the head of the entire noun phrase, and the dimension as the complement (as is explicit in the English construction TWO MONTHS OF VACATION). 

( (NP (NUM drey)
      (N teg)
      (NP-COM (N tsayt))))

( (NP (NUM 340)
      (N meyl)
      (ADJP (ADJ kvadratavi))
      (NP-COM (N platts))))

NP-POS

Possessive NPs are annotated uniformly as NP-POS without distinction as to morphological case - genitive (whether etymologically motivated or not), dative, and so on. For uniformity and ease of retrieval, all possessive NPs, whether consisting of a one word or more, are annotated with a phrasal projection. In principle, possessive NPs can follow the head they modify, but in the corpus, they all precede their head. 
( (NP (NP-POS (D di / der / den) (N yudn))
      (NPR got)))

( (NP (NP-POS (D der) (N$ mamens))
      (N name)))

( (NP (NP-POS (D den) (N$ yegirs))
      (PRO$ zeyn)
      (N zun)))

( (NP (NP-POS (NPR$ gots))
      (N hilf)))

( (NP (NP-POS (NPR bele))
      (PRO$ ir)
      (N tokhter)))

-PRD (primary predicate)

Attaches to ADJP or NP to explicitly indicate when they function as primary predicates. (In the PPCHE, predicate ADJPs are not given a dash tag, and predicate NPs are annotated on a par with ordinary complements as NP-OB1.)

Relative clause (CP-REL)

D- forms or VELKH- forms functioning as relative pronouns (as in modern German) are treated as wh- pronouns. 
( (NP-ACC (D den)
          (N ktb)
          (CP-REL (WNP-1 (WPRO den))
                  (IP-SUB (NP-ACC *T*-1)
                          (NP-SBJ (PRO du))
		          (HVP hast)
                          (NP-DTV (NPR r') (NPR zelikmn))
                          (VBN gshikt)))))

This is not the vernacular type, however. Rather, relative clauses in the vernacular are introduced by the indeclinable complementizer VOS, analogous to English THAT. In Old Yiddish, the complementizer can also be realized as AZ or DZ. 

( (NP-ACC (D di)
	  (N mayse)
	  (PUNC ,)
	  (CP-REL (WNP-1 0)
		  (C vos)
		  (IP-SUB (NP-ACC *T*-1)
			  (NP-SBJ (D der) (N rov))
			  (HVP hot)
			  (NP-DTV (PRO im))
			  (VBN gezogt))))
  (ID 1947E-ROYTE-POMERANTSEN,223.5696))

In Old Yiddish, complementizer VOS is distinguished from the wh- pronoun corresponding to German WAS. In particular, VOS and orthographic variants of it are treated as wh- pronouns in relative clauses modifying quantifier and also in a very few other cases where German would allow WAS (cf. alles, was ich habe, das, was ich habe). In texts after 1800, VOS is invariably treated as a complementizer. 

( (NP-ACC (D daz)
	  (N land)
	  (CP-REL (WNP-1 (WPRO vaz))
		  (IP-SUB (NP-ACC *T*-1)
			  (NP-DTV (PRO unz))
			  (NP-SBJ (NPR gat))
			  (VBN far_shprakhin)
			  (HVP hat))))
  (ID 1666W-MESSIAH,51.249))

( (NP (Q als)
      (CP-REL (WNP-2 (WPRO vaz))
	      (IP-SUB (NP-SBJ *T*-2)
		      (PP (P oyf)
			  (NP (D der) (N erdn)))
		      (VBP iz)
		      (ADVP-LOC (ADVP (ADV dribr))
				(CONJP (CONJ un)
				       (ADVP (ADV drunter)))))))

  (ID 1620E-LEV-TOV-1,4r.6))

As in many vernacular varieties, relative clauses in Yiddish can be "chopped" in the sense of Ross 1967. In other words, an expected preposition more or less closely related to the verb is missing. When the preposition is arguably selected by the verb, such cases are annotated with a silent WPP antecedent. 

( (PP (P oyf)
      (NP (D di)
	  (NUM drey)
	  (N mtsut)
	  (CP-REL (WPP-1 0)
		  (C vs)
		  (IP-SUB (PP *T*-1)
			  (NP-SBJ (PRO mir) (N nsim))
			  (BEP zenn)
			  (RDN varn)
			  (VAN gbatn)))))
  (ID 1712E-SARAH,83.5))

( (NP (D di)
      (N mdinh)
      (NPR haland)
      (CP-REL (WPP-1 0)
	      (C vas)
	      (IP-SUB (NP-PRD (D di)
			      (N hoypt_stat)
			      (PP *T*-1))
		      (VBP iz)
		      (NP-SBJ (NPR amstrdm)
			      (NP-PRN (D dr)
				      (N vaunung)
				      (PP (P fun)
					  (NP (D dem) (N mlkh))))))))
  (ID 1818E-GEOGRAFYE,19.299))

Relative clauses with resumptive elements, which are common in Modern Yiddish, are annotated (as in the PPCHE) by coindexing the wh- antecedent and the resumptive element. 

( (NP-PRD (D a)
	  (N yid)
	  (PUNC ,)
	  (CP-REL (WNP-1 0)
		  (C vos)
		  (IP-SUB (NP-SBJ (PRO me))
			  (VBP ken)
			  (NP-ACC-RSP-1 (PRO im)))))
  (ID 1947E-ROYTE-POMERANTSEN,43.1084))

Title (of book)

Titles of books are indicated with the dash tag -TTL. Very rarely, the syntactic context requires the title to be enclosed as a stand-alone TTL constituent.

VIFL split

Instances of VIFL split are annotated as left-branch extractions (though other analyses are possible). 
( (IP-MAT (NP-SBJ (CP-FRL (WQP-1 (WADV vi@) (Q @fl))
                          (IP-SUB (NP-SBJ (ES es))
                                  (BEP iz)
                                  (VBN geven)
                                  (NP-LGS (QP *T*-1)
                                          (N mentshn))
                                  (PP (P in)
				      (NP (N shif))))))
	  ...))

VOS FAR split

The VOS FAR construction is uniformly annotated as a wh- head VOS with a PP modifier, which is either adjacent to the head or extraposed from it. In other words, the discontinuous case is not treated as left branch extraction. This is consistent with the fact that the construction occurs in varieties of Germanic that do not otherwise exhibit left branch extraction.

The reanalyzed item (VO)SARA is annotated as a wh- determiner (WD), like VELKH-.

WADV+P

See DR+P.

WXP (unknown wh- phrase)

Indicates a wh- phrase whose exact category is impossible to determine because the syntactic context is incomplete (the general case is the antecedent in an incomplete relative clause).

X

Without a leading hyphen. Indicates an unknown lexical category (POS tag).

-X

With a leading hyphen (dash tag). Explicitly indicates that a phrase's grammatical function cannot be determined because the syntactic context is incomplete. The main use in the corpus is as a dummy index on wh- phrases without corresponding traces, as in sluicing.

-XXX

With a leading hyphen (dash tag). Explicitly indicates that a phrase's grammatical function is unclear, though determinable in principle (in contrast to -X).
